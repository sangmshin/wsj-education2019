What is Ambassador?

Ambassador is a new tool in the WSJ University program that allows you to provide your students with a unique link to our order page, track which students subscribe and easily receive your complimentary membership.

How does it work?

After you set up your Ambassador account, you will create a unique URL specifically for you and your students. This URL will link to our order pages, where your students can choose between the WSJ Digital or Print + Digital packs. As they join, their names will appear on your Ambassador dashboard.

After the first student joins, you will receive an email containing a code to redeem your complimentary 6-month WSJ membership. You’ll be able to choose between the WSJ Digital or Print + Digital packs.

In addition, when you refer two of your colleagues to Ambassador, you’ll receive a $25 Amazon gift card after they set up their accounts

Where can I access Ambassador?

You can access Ambassador by visiting WSJuniversity.com and clicking on the XXXXXX tab.

When can I begin using Ambassador?

Ambassador will be available for use prior to the start of the Fall 2017 term.

Who can use Ambassador?

Ambassador is available for college instructors in the U.S. Note that the WSJ Print + Digital pack is available in the contiguous U.S., excluding AK or HI.

Do my students still need to verify that they are students if I use Ambassador?

Yes—each student who purchases an individual WSJ membership will be required to verify that he or she is an active student enrolled in a U.S. college.

What if I do not want to utilize Ambassador—how can my students join?

Your students can still join online at wsj.com/studentoffer.

Will I get credit for orders if I don’t utilize Ambassador?

Professors will no longer earn gifts based on the number of student orders they refer, so there is no need to earn credit for orders. If you choose to not use Ambassador, your students can still join the Journal at WSJ.com/studentoffer.

I will use WSJ in class but don’t want to utilize Ambassador. How can get have complimentary access to WSJ?

If you choose not to utilize Ambassador, please contact your educational representative for assistance with your complimentary membership. To locate your representative, please enter your zip code at http://www.wsjuniversity.com/professors/ and you will be directed to your representative’s contact information.

Can I still get a list of students who subscribe if I don’t utilize Ambassador?

Students will no longer be asked to provide a professor’s name when purchasing a WSJ membership. Ambassador will be the only online method for tracking which students have joined WSJ.

If you choose not to utilize Ambassador, you can ask your students to provide a screenshot of their logged-in WSJ.com homepage, displaying their name in the upper right-hand corner.

Who will help me if I have problems with setting up my Ambassador account?

A set up guide for the Ambassador program can be found at www.wsjuniversity.com/professor.

For additional assistance, please contact your educational representative. To locate your representative, please enter your zip code at http://www.wsjuniversity.com/professors/# and you will be directed to your representative’s contact information.

What are the gifts?

Professor can continue to earn a 6-month complimentary membership and a $25 Amazon.com gift card.

How do I earn them?

You can earn gifts when you use Ambassador.

To earn a complimentary membership, simply create your Ambassador account and share your URL with your students. When the first student joins, we’ll send you a coupon you can use to activate your membership.

To earn the $25 Amazon.com gift card, simply recommend Ambassador to your colleagues. When two of them set up Ambassador accounts, we’ll send your gift card to you.

Is there any other way to earn an Amazon.com gift card?

Effective 8/1/17, the only way to earn a $25 Amazon.com gift card will be to refer colleagues to Ambassador. We’ll send you a gift card when two of your colleagues set up Ambassador accounts.

Why did you change this?

We understand that having a complimentary WSJ membership is critical to successful class integration, and these changes make it easier for you to gain access to WSJ.

I had 10/25+ student orders in the spring/summer term—will I still get my gift(s)?

Professors who had 10+ students join during the spring or summer terms will still receive a complimentary 6-month membership, with a choice of Print + Digital pack or a Digital-only pack.

Professors who had 25+ students join during the spring or summer terms will receive a $25 Amazon gift card. These codes and activation information will be sent out via email. The new Amazon gift card program will become effective on 8/1/17.

Rather than earn a complimentary membership, can I purchase one at educational rates?

Educators can join by visiting subscribe.wsj.com/professor. Please note that a .edu email address is required to complete the purchase.

Why is my complimentary subscription only for 6 months? What do I do if I only teach one semester in a year—do I have to pay for the semester I do not teach?

If your complimentary membership expires when you are on sabbatical or not teaching during a term please contact your educational representative for assistance. To locate your representative, please enter your zip code at http://www.wsjuniversity.com/professors/# and you will be directed to your representative’s contact information.

When is the last date that I can use the manual order forms?

Orders via manual order forms will be accepted and processed through the 7/31/17 expiration date printed on the forms. Professors who submit order forms after 7/31/17 will be notified via Academic Support that the orders will not be processed. Academic Support will recommend that professors utilize Ambassador.

What if students have already purchased bookstore packs and have not activated them yet?

While bookstores will not sell bookstore packs after 7/31/17, students will be able to redeem bookstore packs through 12/31/17.

What do bookstores do with any remaining bookstore packs?

Bookstores can return any unsold bookstore packs to their distributor (either Xanedu or LAD Publishing) per the distributor’s return policy.

Why are you changing the program? Why are you having us (professors) do the work for you?

The WSJ University program is continually evolving to meet the needs of students and faculty. We have found that students prefer ordering online, which gives them immediate access to WSJ. Consequently, we have made the decision to remove the inefficient offline methods.

In addition, professors have indicated they value the ability to view which students have joined WSJ. Through Ambassador’s unique ordering URL, professors will now be able to access their online account and easily view which students have joined, as well as easily earn a complimentary WSJ membership.

When students join online, will they still be required to select the school name and referring professor name?

Effective 8/1/17, each student will be required to select his or her school name, but the name of the referring professor name will no longer be required.

Why are my students automatically signed up for auto-renew when they join online?

All of our subscriptions are on automatic renewal for your students’ convenience. They do not have to worry about mailing back renewal notices or missing issues if they forget to renew. They have complete control over their membership and can contact us at 1-800-Journal to change or cancel at any time.

Joining online requires a credit card for payment—what if my students don’t have one?

Students now have the option of selecting a credit card or PayPal as payment method.
