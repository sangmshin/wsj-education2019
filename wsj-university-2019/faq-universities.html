FAQ

What is The Wall Street Journal?

The Wall Street Journal is a global news organization that provides leading news, information, commentary and analysis. Published by Dow Jones, The Wall Street Journal engages readers across print, digital, mobile, social and video. Building on its heritage as the preeminent source of global business and financial news, the Journal includes coverage of U.S. and world news, politics, culture and more.

What is a WSJ School-Sponsored Membership?

A WSJ School-Sponsored Membership provides a school with a simple way to deliver individual WSJ digital memberships to students, faculty and staff. Each membership includes full WSJ.com access, WSJ mobile and tablet apps and a WSJ+ membership. Also accessible on WSJ.com is a 90-day archive of the print paper.

Individual WSJ digital memberships allow users to do company research, follow comprehensive global news, track financial markets, receive newsletters and much more. There are also teaching tools and resources available for faculty to help integrate content into the curriculum.

What are the benefits of a WSJ School-Sponsored Membership?

    Secure login to WSJ.com, plus mobile and tablet apps Integration of WSJ.com content into your Learning Management System Easy implementation, use and data reporting WSJ marketing support through search and social to increase program awareness and activation Individual personalization for students No direct expense for students

How does The Wall Street Journal help push activation among students, staff and faculty?

When an institution signs up for a school-sponsored membership, WSJ will provide support to encourage student activation, including customized marketing materials for you to promote through your university’s channels. Additionally, WSJ will provide a dedicated WSJ contact to support implementation, provide marketing materials and direct any service-related questions.

What are the implementation options?

All WSJ site license schools receive a unique partnership URL, which their students, faculty and staff can register digital WSJ access. The process in which we set up this URL and how we authenticate users is called implementation. This process typically takes 2-3 weeks for new site license schools. WSJ's preferred implementation method is through the school's single sign-on (SSO) portal. This method authenticates users through the school's single sign-on platform.

If your university cannot support SSO at this time, we will work to set up your university via our alternative implementation method.

How long does a typical implementation take?

Once an implementation option is selected, the average implementation takes 2–3 weeks for new clients.

When a university subscribes, what content archive is included?

The school-sponsored education membership gives a four-year archive for electronic content. WSJ.com is available in the U.S., Asia, Europe and India editions, and is easily convertible into Chinese and Japanese languages. It is the same full access that any regular consumer would subscribe to, just at a very low education price. To learn more about The Wall Street Journal, please visit dowjones.com/products/wsj/.

How does a school access content preceding the four-year electronic archive?

If schools are looking for WSJ access previous to the four-year archive, another Dow Jones product, Factiva, has the WSJ archive back to 1979. To learn more about Factiva, please visit dowjones.com/products/factiva/.

What is searchable on WSJ.com?

In the “Advanced Search,” readers can search WSJ articles, blogs, videos and press releases. The search allows for up to a four-year archive of results.

Can interested universities have trial access?

WSJ can issue up to 10 individual digital-trial memberships per institution for decision makers. This is for interested universities/libraries to review the product. Trial access is complimentary.

Can a university license for individual departments only, such as a business school?

Our university wide pricing is our highest volume discount and aims to meet a growing need to serve all students with a credible and personalized news source at an affordable price. There is an option to restrict access to a specific college/school at a slightly higher rate per user. WSJ can provide a comparison of the costs per individual school and university wide.

How do schools receive quotes and additional information for school-sponsored memberships?

Schools may request more information from their WSJ representative by completing the “Contact Your Rep” section on this site.

How long is a subscription term and when does it start?

A university can start at any date. The standard contract runs one calendar year.

What is the pricing basis?

Standard pricing is based on the university’s student headcount.

Are WSJ-School Sponsored Memberships available outside of the U.S.?

Yes, they are available internationally. Please contact partnersubs@dowjones.com for more details.
